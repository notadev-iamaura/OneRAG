# Code of Conduct

OneRAG is built by people with different backgrounds, languages, and levels of
experience. We expect collaboration to stay respectful, direct, and focused on
the work.

## Expected Behavior

- Be kind and professional, especially when giving critical feedback.
- Assume good intent, but clarify quickly when a thread becomes confusing.
- Keep discussion technical, specific, and evidence-based.
- Respect maintainers' time by providing reproduction steps, logs, and context.
- Avoid sharing private data, secrets, or third-party confidential information.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, or sustained personal attacks.
- Discriminatory language or exclusionary behavior.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Deliberately derailing issues, reviews, or support channels.
- Submitting malicious code, exploit payloads, or secrets.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments; close issues; reject pull
requests; or block participants who violate this policy. Serious security or
safety issues should be reported privately using the process in `SECURITY.md`.

## Scope

This code applies to OneRAG repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions,
documentation, community channels, and project-related events.
